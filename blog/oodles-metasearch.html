Oodles: The Ant That Crawls for You

In the vast wilderness of the internet, one tiny creature is working overtime to bring you the best search results possible. Meet Oodles, the metasearch engine with the heart of an ant.

Oodles isn’t just another search engine, it is a homemade engine built to gather results from Brave, Bing and Yahooall in one place. That means less bias, more variety and a broader view of the web. Oodles delivers with precision.

Say hello to our mascot: the ant. Small but mighty, just like our crawler.

We chose the ant as our mascot for a reason. Ants are nature’s original crawlers. They are relentless, organized and efficient. That’s exactly how Oodles works. It doesn’t track you. It just crawls, collects and delivers clean results from multiple sources.

Unlike traditional search engines that rely on a single index, metasearch engines like Oodles pull results from multiple providers. That means you’re not stuck in one algorithm’s bubble. You get a richer, more balanced view of the web. It’s like having three librarians instead of one, each offering their best picks.

Oodles is also designed to work with Ringzauber, our private browser built with Python. In fact, Oodles may become the default search engine for Ringzauber, bringing metasearch power directly into your browsing experience. Together, they form a privacy-first duo that respects your time, your data and your curiosity.

Oodles includes AI analysis and voice search, making it smarter and more accessible than ever. And while image search is still a work in progress, we’re building it with the same care and privacy-first mindset.

So why settle for one search engine’s version of the truth? With Oodles, you get a mosaic of perspectives!
